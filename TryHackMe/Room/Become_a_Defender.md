## 1) Context
Short summary: Explore defensive security, cyber infrastructure, and how to protect systems from attacks. The lab introduces key defensive concepts, including visibility, prevention, detection, mitigation, and defense in depth.  

## 2) Initial hypothesis
- Understand what defenders protect and why visibility matters  
- Identify key parts of a client's environment  
- Learn basic methods used to protect common systems and services  
- Feel confident starting your defensive security journey  

## 3) Tools used
- None required for theory, but concepts include monitoring tools, firewalls, logging systems  

## 4) Approach (high level)
- Map the environment and assets of an organization  
- Understand how systems communicate and interact  
- Identify potential attack paths and areas needing protection  
- Apply city analogy to illustrate defensive roles and responsibilities  
- Explore core functions: prevention, detection, mitigation, analysis, and improvement  

## 5) Results / Evidence (sanitized)
- Gained conceptual understanding of defensive security principles  
- Identified key components of infrastructure: employee devices, servers, mail systems, firewalls, internet exposure  
- Learned how attackers exploit progressive attack chains and how defenders can disrupt them  
- Applied the city analogy to relate defensive actions to real-world examples  

## 6) Recommended remediation
- Implement preventive controls: firewalls, antivirus, patch management  
- Establish monitoring and detection systems: logging, alerts, traffic analysis  
- Develop mitigation plans: isolate compromised systems, block malicious IPs  
- Use layered defenses (defense in depth) to protect high-value assets  
- Continuously review and improve security posture after incidents  

## 7) Lessons learned
- Security begins with **visibility** — unknown systems cannot be defended  
- Mapping and understanding infrastructure is essential before deploying protections  
- Systems are **interconnected**; compromise in one can lead to access to others  
- Defensive security is a **continuous cycle**: prevention, detection, mitigation, analysis, improvement  
- Attackers operate via **progressive attack chains**; early detection is key  
- **Defense in depth** provides multiple layers of protection across infrastructure  
- High-value assets need **stronger protections and monitoring**  
- Anticipating attacker behavior is central to effective defense
